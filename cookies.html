1. What Are Cookies? A Deep Dive into Digital Crumbs

Cookies are small text files that are placed on your computer or mobile device when you visit a website. They are widely used to make websites work more efficiently, as well as to provide reporting information and to remember information about you, such as your login details or language preferences. Think of them as tiny digital notes that a website leaves on your device to remember you for future visits. These notes allow the website to recognize your browser and capture certain information.

They are not programs and cannot carry viruses or install malware. Cookies are simply data files that store information about your browsing session. This information can be retrieved by the website that placed the cookie during subsequent visits. This capability is fundamental to modern web browsing, enabling features like shopping carts, personalized content, and user authentication without requiring you to re-enter information repeatedly. The data within a cookie is typically a string of text and numbers, which acts as a unique identifier for your browser.

3. Types of Cookies We Use: A Detailed Breakdown

Cookies come in various forms, each serving a distinct purpose. Understanding these different types will give you a clearer picture of how they operate on Yumckify and contribute to your overall user experience.

3.1. Session Cookies

Session cookies are temporary cookies that remain on your device only for the duration of your browsing session. They are automatically deleted from your device when you close your web browser. These cookies are essential for the proper functioning of the Site during a single visit. For instance, they might remember items you've added to a shopping cart or keep you logged in as you navigate between pages. They do not collect any information from your computer and are used solely to facilitate your current visit to the Site. Their transient nature means they are primarily used for critical, short-term functionalities.

3.2. Persistent Cookies

Persistent cookies, also known as "permanent" or "stored" cookies, remain on your device for a longer period, even after you close your browser. They have an expiration date and will be deleted automatically when that date is reached, or they can be manually deleted by you. These cookies are used to remember your preferences or actions across multiple visits to a website. Examples include remembering your language preference, login details so you don't have to re-enter them every time, or specific customization settings. They allow for a more personalized and convenient experience by recalling your choices from previous sessions.

3.3. First-Party Cookies

First-party cookies are cookies set by the website you are visiting (i.e., Yumckify.com). These cookies are generally used to improve your experience on our Site, such as remembering your preferences, managing your login session, or enabling core functionalities. Since they are set by us, we have direct control over the data they collect and how it is used. They are integral to the Site's operational efficiency and user-centric design, ensuring that your interactions are smooth and tailored to your needs.

3.4. Third-Party Cookies

Third-party cookies are cookies set by a domain other than the one you are visiting. These are typically used for cross-site tracking, ad serving, and analytics by external services integrated into the website. For example, if we use Google Analytics, Google sets a third-party cookie to collect data on our behalf. Similarly, social media sharing buttons or embedded videos from platforms like YouTube might set third-party cookies. While we choose to integrate these services, the cookies themselves are controlled by the respective third parties and are subject to their own privacy policies. These cookies enable functionalities that enhance the Site's offerings, such as providing detailed analytics or facilitating social sharing.

4.25. Pixel Tags / Web Beacons

5. Your Choices: Managing Your Cookie Preferences

You have the power to control and manage cookies on your device. While most web browsers automatically accept cookies, you can usually modify your browser settings to decline cookies or to notify you when a cookie is being placed on your computer. Please be aware that blocking or deleting cookies may impact your experience on our Site and other websites, as some features may not function correctly.

5.1. Browser Settings

You can manage cookies through your web browser settings. Most browsers allow you to:

  • Block all cookies: This will prevent any website from placing cookies on your device.
  • Block third-party cookies: This will only block cookies from domains other than the one you are visiting.
  • Receive alerts: Your browser can notify you when a website tries to set a cookie, allowing you to accept or decline it manually.
  • Delete existing cookies: You can clear all stored cookies from your browser history.

Please note that if you disable cookies, some features of the Yumckify Site may not function as intended. For example, you might not be able to stay logged in, or personalized recommendations may not appear.
